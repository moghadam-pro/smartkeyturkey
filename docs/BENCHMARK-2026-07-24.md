# SmartKeyTurkey experience benchmark — 24 July 2026

## Scope

This benchmark reviews patterns relevant to SmartKeyTurkey's two acquisition journeys:

- international B2C property discovery in Türkiye;
- B2B petrochemical discovery and qualified RFQ submission.

It is a product and content benchmark, not an endorsement of competitor claims. Time-sensitive legal, investment, supplier-verification and performance claims must be checked independently before SmartKeyTurkey adopts similar language.

## Reference set

| Reference | Relevant pattern | SmartKeyTurkey takeaway |
|---|---|---|
| [Property Turkey](https://www.propertyturkey.com/) | Large property inventory, city/lifestyle segmentation, extensive buyer guides and visible expertise content | Build topical depth around verified buyer questions, but keep dates, sources and review status visible so older guidance does not appear current indefinitely |
| [Antalya Homes](https://antalyahomes.com/) | Multi-office trust, international-buyer services, multilingual reach and pre-due-diligence positioning | Use operational proof and a clear buying process; defer multilingual expansion until English templates and claims pass review |
| [Maximos Real Estate](https://www.realestateallturkey.com/) | Verification language, office/history proof, legal-first guidance and strong consultation calls to action | Show exactly what SmartKeyTurkey reviews and avoid generic “trusted” claims without evidence |
| [ChemPortal](https://chemportal.com.tr/) | Category-led petrochemical discovery, product search, technical-detail pages and direct quote requests | Preserve family navigation and RFQ-first conversion; add stronger document, packaging, destination and application qualification |
| [TurkIndustry](https://www.turkindustry.com/en) | Supplier/category discovery, product catalogs, RFQ creation and company comparison | Make the catalog searchable and structured, while differentiating SmartKeyTurkey as a managed representative rather than an open supplier marketplace |
| [PortYol](https://portyol.com/) | Three-step sourcing explanation and a prominent “one request” value proposition | Explain the sourcing workflow before asking for detailed RFQ information and set expectations about confirmation of availability and commercial terms |
| [Invest in Türkiye property guide](https://beta.invest.gov.tr/en/investmentguide/pages/acquiring-property-and-citizenship.aspx) | Official emphasis on title registration, restrictions, debts, appointments and transaction checks | Anchor property guidance in primary official sources and separate general education from transaction-specific legal review |

## Pattern comparison

| Dimension | Strong market pattern | Current SmartKeyTurkey position | Decision |
|---|---|---|---|
| First-screen clarity | One audience, one primary promise and one conversion action | Homepage serves both property and petrochemical audiences | Keep the dual-path hero, but make the B2C/B2B split immediate and preserve one primary CTA inside each path |
| Trust | Years, offices, verified inventory, expert profiles, guides and process proof | Direct-control property disclosure, representative petrochemical disclosure, sourced editorial content | Add evidence-backed trust modules; do not invent transaction counts, certifications, partners or “verified” claims |
| Property discovery | City/type filters, large inventory and buyer-purpose landing pages | Four phase-one cities, sale/rent state, structured facts and no public prices | Retain request-only terms; add verified inventory progressively and build intent pages only when enough real listings exist |
| Property risk reduction | Buying guides, title/legal explanations and consultation | Source/review metadata and property verification fields | Show “last reviewed”, document status and due-diligence prompts consistently; link legal claims to official guidance |
| Petrochemical discovery | Search, family navigation, technical details and RFQ | 99 products, 13 families, structured details and context-aware RFQ | Add prominent product search and document/application cues; keep price and availability available on qualified request only |
| RFQ qualification | Quantity, specification, destination and timeline | Product, quantity, unit, packaging, Incoterm, destination, documents, date and attachment | Current field set is competitive; reduce perceived effort with a short three-step explanation and progress-oriented grouping |
| Content | High-volume guides and market updates | Sourced English editorial workflow with review status | Prefer lower-volume, primary-source content with freshness controls over unsourced publishing volume |
| Localization | Many languages and localized sales teams | English phase one; WPML deferred | Maintain localization-ready fields and templates, but do not launch languages before human review capacity exists |
| Commercial transparency | Prices or “from” prices are common in property; B2B usually asks for quote | No public property or product prices | State “commercial terms on request” consistently so missing prices do not look accidental |

## Recommended implementation priorities

### P0 — trust and role clarity

1. Keep the property and petrochemical roles visibly separate:
   - properties and projects under SmartKeyTurkey's direct control;
   - authorized petrochemical sales representation, not manufacturing.
2. Add a reusable “What we verify / What must still be confirmed” component.
3. Display source, reviewed date and pending-human-review state on time-sensitive guidance.
4. Prohibit unsupported claims about returns, guaranteed citizenship, verified suppliers, delivery, stock or transaction volume.

### P1 — conversion journeys

1. Add catalog search that works alongside the product-family sidebar.
2. Group the petrochemical RFQ visually into buyer, product, delivery and documentation sections without changing its database-only delivery.
3. Add property intent entry points for sale and rent when verified inventory supports them.
4. Place a concise three-step process immediately before each long request form.
5. Use outcome-specific CTA labels such as “Request product availability” and “Request a property consultation”.

### P1 — SEO and content

1. Create unique descriptions and internal links for product-family pages before indexing empty/thin taxonomy pages.
2. Expand city pages only with verified local inventory and city-specific guidance.
3. Maintain an editorial refresh queue for laws, statistics and events.
4. Use official sources for legal/transaction guidance and distinguish facts from SmartKeyTurkey interpretation.

### P2 — later growth experiments

1. Test short campaign landing pages by city and intent instead of sending paid traffic to broad archives.
2. Test a saved/shortlisted property flow only after real inventory volume justifies it.
3. Test a lightweight RFQ start that reveals the full qualification form after product selection.
4. Add multilingual acquisition only after English conversion data and human review capacity are available.

## Risks to avoid

- Mixing property ownership/control language with the petrochemical representative role.
- Copying competitor “trusted”, “verified”, citizenship or return claims without auditable evidence.
- Publishing high-volume AI guidance without source, date and human-review state.
- Creating thin city, taxonomy or campaign pages only for keyword coverage.
- Treating an RFQ as a confirmed quote, stock position or delivery commitment.
- Adding multilingual pages before operational response and editorial review exist in those languages.

## Next decision gate

The positioning/KPI step should turn this benchmark into:

- one value proposition per audience;
- approved proof points and prohibited claims;
- CTA hierarchy by page type;
- baseline metrics for qualified property inquiries, qualified petrochemical RFQs, form completion, organic landing-page engagement and source freshness.
